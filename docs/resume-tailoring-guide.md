# Resume Tailoring Guide

## Goal

Tailor the master PM resume in 15 to 20 minutes without rewriting it from scratch.

## Default process

1. Read the job description once for role shape.
2. Read it again and highlight customer, domain, and delivery language.
3. Update the headline and summary so the first sentence mirrors the role's actual problem space.
4. Reorder top bullets so the most relevant outcomes appear first.
5. Add only keywords that are backed by real evidence.

## What can change safely

- Headline
- Summary paragraph
- Top 2 to 4 bullets across the most relevant roles
- Skills emphasis
- Selected project names

## What should stay stable

- Core transition story
- Metrics and verified outcomes
- Company names, dates, and job titles
- Featured case studies in the public portfolio

## Tailoring patterns

### For general PM roles

- Lead with product strategy, prioritization, and customer impact.
- Keep technical language present but secondary.

### For technical PM roles

- Emphasize engineering fluency, systems thinking, and delivery risk management.
- Keep customer value visible so the resume does not collapse into engineering identity.

### For product owner or hybrid roles

- Stress backlog refinement, agile ceremonies, issue shaping, and stakeholder alignment.
- Use Lambda Curry and HSNBA higher in the story.

## Final check before sending

- Is Product Manager or a close variant visible in the headline?
- Is there a metric or concrete outcome in the top half?
- Does the summary sound like the target role, not a generic background paragraph?
- Do the top bullets support the same story as the portfolio homepage?
